विद्युत आवेश तथा क्षेत्र (Electric Charges and Fields)

1.1 विद्युत आवेश (Electric Charge)

विद्युत आवेश पदार्थ का एक मौलिक गुण है। यह दो प्रकार के होते हैं: धनात्मक (positive) और ऋणात्मक (negative)।

1.2 स्थिर विद्युत (Electrostatics)

जब विद्युत आवेश गतिशील न होकर स्थिर होता है, तो उस स्थिति में उसके द्वारा उत्पन्न प्रभावों का अध्ययन स्थिर विद्युत में किया जाता है।

1.3 कूलॉम्ब का नियम (Coulomb's Law)

दो बिंदु आवेशों के बीच लगने वाला बल उनके आवेशों के गुणनफल के अनुक्रमानुपाती होता है तथा उनके बीच की दूरी के वर्ग के व्युत्क्रमानुपाती होता है। 

F = k * (q1 * q2) / r²

जहाँ, k = 9 × 10⁹ Nm²/C² होता है।

Introduction

Motion is a change in the position of an object with respect to time. It can be described in terms of displacement, velocity, acceleration, and time.

Types of Motion

  • Rectilinear Motion: Motion along a straight line.
  • Circular Motion: Motion along a circular path.
  • Periodic Motion: Motion that repeats after equal intervals of time.

Important Formulas

  • Speed = Distance / Time
  • Velocity = Displacement / Time
  • Acceleration = (Final velocity - Initial velocity) / Time
  • v = u + at
  • s = ut + ½at²
  • v² = u² + 2as

Introduction

Electrostatics deals with the forces, fields, and potentials arising from static electric charges. In this chapter, we study the concept of electrostatic potential and capacitance.

Key Concepts

  • Electrostatic Potential (V)
  • Potential due to a Point Charge
  • Equipotential Surfaces
  • Capacitance and Capacitors
  • Parallel Plate Capacitor
  • Effect of Dielectric on Capacitance
  • Energy Stored in a Capacitor

Important Formulas

  • Electrostatic Potential: \( V = \frac{kQ}{r} \)
  • Capacitance: \( C = \frac{Q}{V} \)
  • Capacitance of parallel plate capacitor: \( C = \frac{\varepsilon_0 A}{d} \)
  • Energy Stored: \( U = \frac{1}{2}CV^2 \)
  • With dielectric: \( C = \frac{K\varepsilon_0 A}{d} \)

Physics - Chapter 3: Current Electricity

Home Chapter 2 Chapter 3

Introduction

Current electricity is the study of electric charges in motion. It deals with how current flows in conductors, resistance, electromotive force (emf), and electric circuits.

Key Concepts

  • Electric Current (I)
  • Drift Velocity
  • Ohm’s Law
  • Resistivity and Conductivity
  • Combination of Resistors: Series and Parallel
  • Electromotive Force (emf) and Internal Resistance
  • Kirchhoff's Laws
  • Wheatstone Bridge
  • Meter Bridge
  • Potentiometer

Important Formulas

  • Current: \( I = \frac{Q}{t} \)
  • Ohm's Law: \( V = IR \)
  • Resistivity: \( \rho = R \frac{A}{l} \)
  • Drift Velocity: \( v_d = \frac{I}{nAe} \)
  • Power: \( P = VI = I^2R = \frac{V^2}{R} \)
  • Combination of Resistors:
    • Series: \( R_{eq} = R_1 + R_2 + ... \)
    • Parallel: \( \frac{1}{R_{eq}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + ... \)
  • Kirchhoff's Laws:
    • Junction Rule: \( \sum I_{in} = \sum I_{out} \)
    • Loop Rule: \( \sum V = 0 \) in a closed loop

Physics - Chapter 4: Moving Charges and Magnetism

Home Chapter 2 Chapter 3 Chapter 4

Introduction

This chapter deals with the magnetic effects produced by moving charges or currents. It includes the study of magnetic forces, magnetic field, motion of charged particles, and magnetic field due to current-carrying conductors.

Key Concepts

  • Magnetic Force on Moving Charge
  • Lorentz Force
  • Motion of Charged Particle in Magnetic Field
  • Biot–Savart Law
  • Magnetic Field on Axis of Circular Loop
  • Ampere’s Circuital Law
  • Force Between Two Parallel Currents
  • Torque on Current Loop
  • Moving Coil Galvanometer

Important Formulas

  • Magnetic Force: \( \vec{F} = q\vec{v} \times \vec{B} \)
  • Force on Current-Carrying Wire: \( \vec{F} = I\vec{L} \times \vec{B} \)
  • Biot–Savart Law: \( d\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \cdot \frac{Id\vec{l} \times \hat{r}}{r^2} \)
  • Ampere’s Law: \( \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I \)
  • Magnetic Field at Centre of Circular Loop: \( B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2R} \)
  • Force Between Parallel Wires: \( F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 l}{2\pi d} \)
  • Torque on Coil: \( \tau = NIBA\sin\theta \)

Physics - Chapter 4: Moving Charges and Magnetism

Home Chapter 2 Chapter 3 Chapter 4

Introduction

This chapter explores the magnetic effects caused by moving electric charges (current). It includes the concepts of magnetic field, force on a moving charge, Biot–Savart law, Ampere’s law, and applications like galvanometers and force between current-carrying wires.

Key Concepts

  • Magnetic field and Magnetic force
  • Lorentz force: Combination of electric and magnetic forces
  • Motion of charged particle in uniform magnetic field
  • Biot–Savart Law
  • Magnetic field due to straight wire, circular loop, and solenoid
  • Ampere’s Circuital Law
  • Force between two parallel current-carrying wires
  • Torque on a current loop in magnetic field
  • Moving coil galvanometer – construction & working

Important Formulas

  • Magnetic Force: \( \vec{F} = q(\vec{v} \times \vec{B}) \)
  • Force on Current-Carrying Wire: \( \vec{F} = I\vec{L} \times \vec{B} \)
  • Biot–Savart Law: \( d\vec{B} = \frac{\mu_0}{4\pi} \cdot \frac{Id\vec{l} \times \hat{r}}{r^2} \)
  • Ampere’s Law: \( \oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0 I \)
  • Magnetic Field at Center of Circular Loop: \( B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2R} \)
  • Force Between Parallel Currents: \( F = \frac{\mu_0 I_1 I_2 l}{2\pi d} \)
  • Torque on a Coil: \( \tau = NIBA \sin\theta \)
  • Magnetic Dipole Moment: \( M = NIA \)
